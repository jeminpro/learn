# 🧠 Creative Writing Genres

---
## 🧠 CORE RULE (applies to ALL genres)

**Before writing anything:**

⏱️ **5-minute plan:**
- What is the task?
- Who is the audience?
- What is the structure?

👉 If this step is skipped → marks drop immediately

---

## ✍️ 1. STORY WRITING (Most common)

### 🎯 Goal:
Clear, simple story with structure — NOT complicated ideas

### ✅ Use this structure: CAPRE
- **Character** → Who?
- **Action** → What’s happening?
- **Problem** → What goes wrong?
- **Resolution** → How is it solved?
- **Ending** → Final feeling/result

### 🧱 How to write (step-by-step)

**🔹 Paragraph 1 – Start in action**
- ❌ “It was a sunny day…”
- ✅ “Arjun ran down the empty street, his heart pounding.”

**🔹 Paragraph 2 – Problem**
Something must go wrong:
- Lost item
- Fear
- Conflict

**🔹 Paragraph 3 – Resolution**
Character acts → solves or faces problem

**🔹 Paragraph 4 – Ending**
- Reflect
- Link back to beginning
- Leave a feeling

### ⭐ What examiners want:
- Clear structure
- Logical flow
- Finished ending

### ❌ Avoid:
- Too many characters
- Random events
- “It was all a dream”

---

## 📝 2. FORMAL LETTER

### 🎯 Goal:
Clear, polite, structured communication

### 🧱 Structure:
Dear Sir/Madam,

Paragraph 1: Why are you writing

Paragraph 2: Details / explanation

Paragraph 3: What you want to happen

Yours sincerely,
[Name]


### 🧠 Key rules:
- No slang  
- No contractions (don’t → do not)  
- Clear and respectful tone  

### ✅ Example tone:
“I am writing to express my concern about…”

### ❌ Avoid:
- Chatty language  
- Long messy paragraphs  

---

## 💬 3. INFORMAL LETTER

### 🎯 Goal:
Friendly, natural tone

### 🧱 Structure:
Hi [Name],

Paragraph 1: Why are you writing?

Paragraph 2: Main content

Paragraph 3: Ending

From,
[Name]


### 🧠 Key rules:
- Can use contractions  
- Sounds like talking to a friend  
- Still organised  

---

## 📓 4. DIARY ENTRY

### 🎯 Goal:
Personal thoughts + feelings

### 🧱 Structure:
Date

Dear Diary,

Paragraph 1: What happened

Paragraph 2: Key moment

Paragraph 3: Feelings / reflection


### 🧠 Key rules:
- First person (“I…”)  
- Emotional language  
- Honest voice  

### ❌ Avoid:
- Writing like a story  
- Forgetting feelings  

---

## 🖼️ 5. DESCRIPTION

### 🎯 Goal:
Create a picture in the reader’s mind

### 🧱 Structure:
- **Paragraph 1:** Set the scene  
- **Paragraph 2:** Zoom into details  
- **Paragraph 3:** Change mood or perspective  

### 🧠 Use:
- **Senses:**
  - What can you see?
  - Hear?
  - Feel?

### ✅ Example:
“The wind whispered through the empty trees…”

### ❌ Avoid:
- Listing adjectives  
  “big, dark, scary, creepy forest…”

---

## 🎭 6. APPLIED COMPREHENSION (Write in role)

### 🎯 Goal:
Read a passage → write as if you are inside that story

### 🧠 Before writing:
Ask:
- Who am I?
- How do I feel?
- What do I want?

### 🧱 Structure depends on task:
- Diary → feelings  
- Letter → communication  
- Speech → persuasion  

### ⚠️ Key:
Stay consistent with character voice  

---

## ⚖️ 7. DISCURSIVE WRITING

### 🎯 Goal:
Discuss BOTH sides

### 🧱 Structure:
- Paragraph 1: Introduction  
- Paragraph 2: Side 1  
- Paragraph 3: Side 2  
- Paragraph 4: Conclusion  

### 🧠 Use phrases:
- “On the one hand…”  
- “However…”  
- “In contrast…”  

### ❌ Avoid:
- Taking strong sides (unless asked)  

---

## 📢 8. PERSUASIVE WRITING

### 🎯 Goal:
Convince the reader

### 🧱 Use PREP:
- **Point** → Your opinion  
- **Reason** → Why  
- **Evidence** → Example  
- **Point again** → Stronger restatement  

### 🧠 Use:
- Strong language  
- Clear arguments  
- Call to action  

### ✅ Example:
“We must act now…”

### ❌ Avoid:
- Weak arguments  
- No examples  

---

## 🧾 9. NEWS REPORT (Less Common)

### 🎯 Goal:
Clear, factual reporting

### 🧱 Structure:
- Headline  
- First paragraph: Who, What, Where, When  
- Next paragraphs: Details  
- Include quotes  

### 🧠 Tone:
- Neutral  
- Informative  

---

## 🗂️ 10. INSTRUCTIONS (Less Common)

### 🎯 Goal:
Clear steps

### 🧱 Structure:
1. Step one  
2. Step two  
3. Step three  

### 🧠 Use:
- Command verbs:
  - “Cut”  
  - “Mix”  
  - “Place”  

---

## 🎤 11. SPEECH (Less Common)

### 🎯 Goal:
Be spoken aloud

### 🧱 Structure:
- Opening: “Good morning…”  
- Points  
- Ending: Call to action  

### 🧠 Use:
- Rhetorical questions  
- Repetition  

---

## 📰 12. SCHOOL MAGAZINE

### 🎯 Goal:
Light, informative article for pupils and parents

### 🧱 Structure:
- Clear heading  
- Short subheadings  
- Introduction (hook + topic)  
- 2–3 focused paragraphs  
- Closing line  

### 🧠 Tone:
- Semi-formal  
- Friendly but informative  

### ❌ Avoid:
- Too formal (like an essay)  
- Too casual (like a chat)  

---

## 🎬 13. PLAYSCRIPT

### 🎯 Goal:
Write dialogue for performance

### 🧱 Structure:
(Opening stage direction – setting + mood)

Character: Dialogue
Character: Dialogue

(Short stage directions where needed)


### 🧠 Key rules:
- Use speaker labels  
- Keep dialogue on separate lines  
- Minimal narration  

### ❌ Avoid:
- Long paragraphs  
- Over-explaining actions  

---

## 📖 14. RECOUNT

### 🎯 Goal:
Chronological retelling of an event

### 🧱 Structure:
- Paragraph 1: Set context (who/where/when)  
- Paragraph 2: Events in order  
- Paragraph 3: Ending / reflection  

### 🧠 Use:
- Time connectives:  
  “First… Then… Later… Finally…”  
- First OR third person (stay consistent)  

### ❌ Avoid:
- Jumping between events  
- Changing tense/person  

---

## 🔥 Final Insight (Most Important)

Examiners are NOT thinking:
> “Wow, what an amazing idea”

They are thinking:
- Is it structured?  
- Is it clear?  
- Is it finished?  
- Is it controlled?
