# Related Numbers

### What you are asked to do

You are given **groups of three numbers** like this:

**(a [ b ] c)**

* The numbers **outside the brackets** are related to the **number inside the brackets**
* **Each group uses the SAME rule**
* You must find the **missing middle number** in the final group

Example:
(4 [10] 6) (13 [27] 14)
(16 [ ? ] 8)

Rule: add the two outside numbers
4 + 6 = 10
13 + 14 = 27
16 + 8 = **24**

Answer: **24**

---

## How to solve (step-by-step)

1. Look at the **first complete group**
2. Work out how the middle number is made from the two outside numbers

   * add?
   * subtract?
   * multiply?
   * double then add?
3. Check the rule works for the **second group**
4. Apply the same rule to the final group

---

## Tips

* 🔍 Always start with the **simplest rule** (add or subtract)
* 🔢 If addition doesn’t work, try:

  * difference (larger − smaller)
  * double one number
  * multiply then add/subtract
* ❌ Ignore complicated rules unless simple ones fail
* ✅ The rule must work for **all groups**
