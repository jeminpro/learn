**Application:**
- choose most important skill for an activity
- understand why a skill is needed

## 1. 🧠 Core Fitness Components (VERY HIGH PRIORITY)

These are **perfect MCQ targets**.

### Teach clearly:

* **Strength** → how much force muscles can produce
* **Stamina / Endurance** → ability to keep going for a long time
* **Flexibility** → how much joints can move
* **Balance** → staying stable
* **Coordination** → using body parts together smoothly
* **Agility** → changing direction quickly
* **Speed** → how fast you move

### Likely Questions

* “Which word means ability to keep going for a long time?” → **Endurance**
* “Which skill helps you change direction quickly?” → **Agility**

---

## 2. ❤️ Body & Exercise (High Yield)

### Teach:

* Exercise increases:

  * **heart rate**
  * **breathing rate**
* Muscles:

  * contract (tighten)
  * relax

### Vocabulary

* **heart rate**
* **pulse**
* **muscles**
* **oxygen**
* **energy**

### Likely Questions

* “What happens to heart rate during exercise?” → **Increases**
* “Which organ pumps blood?” → **Heart**

---

## 3. 🏃 Types of Activities (Recognition-Based)

They won’t ask rules — but they may test **classification**.

### Categories:

* **Games** (football, cricket) → teamwork
* **Gymnastics** → balance, control
* **Dance** → rhythm, expression
* **Athletics** → running, jumping, throwing
* **Outdoor activities** → problem-solving

### Likely Questions

* “Which activity focuses on rhythm?” → **Dance**
* “Which activity includes running and jumping?” → **Athletics**

# ⚠️ What NOT to Spend Time On

Avoid:

* Detailed sport rules ❌
* History of sports ❌
* Exact measurements (court sizes etc.) ❌

These are **not FSCE-friendly**.

---

# 🎯 How to Teach (Very Important)

Since it's MCQ / one-word:

### Use this method:

* Teach → Define → Test immediately

Example:

* Ask: “What is flexibility?”
* Then MCQ:

  * A. Speed
  * B. Strength
  * C. Movement of joints ✅
