# What is included?
## History (FSCE)

### 1. Time & Chronology
- past, present, future
- timeline, chronology

### 2. Cause & Effect
- cause (why), effect (result)

### 3. Evidence & Sources
- primary, secondary

### 4. Similarity & Difference
- compare past and present

### 5. Evaluating Evidence (IMPORTANT)
- usefulness and reliability

**Vocabulary:**
- evidence, source
- primary, secondary
- reliable, useful, accurate

**Application:**
- identify most useful evidence
- recognise unreliable sources
- compare types of sources


# 🏛️ History for 11+ FSCE (MCQ / One-Word Focus)

---

# 🟩 1. Time & Chronology (VERY HIGH PRIORITY)

This is the **foundation** — very testable.

## Concepts

* **past** → already happened

* **present** → now

* **future** → yet to happen

* Ordering events:

  * earlier / later
  * before / after

---

## Vocabulary

* **past**
* **present**
* **future**
* **timeline**
* **chronology**

---

## Likely Questions

* “Events in order of time is called?” → **Chronology**
* “Something that already happened?” → **Past**

---

## Vocabulary

# 🟧 4. Evidence & Sources (VERY HIGH PRIORITY)

This is one of the **most testable areas**.

## Concepts

* We learn about history using **evidence**

### Types:

* **Primary source** → from the time (diary, photo)
* **Secondary source** → later (books, reports)

---

## Vocabulary

* **evidence**
* **source**
* **primary**
* **secondary**

---

## Likely Questions

* “A diary written at the time is?” → **Primary source**
* “Information from books written later?” → **Secondary source**

# ⚠️ What NOT to Teach

Avoid:
❌ exact dates
❌ detailed events (e.g., Vikings, Romans specifics)
❌ memorising timelines

These are **low return for FSCE**.

---

# 🎯 What FSCE Will Actually Test

They will test:
✅ understanding of words (chronology, evidence)
✅ simple reasoning (cause/effect)
✅ recognising types of sources
✅ basic concepts about past vs present

---

# 🧠 Teaching Strategy (Very Important)

Use **quick question style**:

* “Is a photograph from 1900 primary or secondary?”
* “If something happens because of another event, what is that called?”

Keep it:
👉 fast
👉 repetitive
👉 vocabulary-focused

---

# ✔️ Final Structure

Teach History in these 8 buckets:

1. Time & chronology
2. Change & continuity
3. Cause & effect
4. Evidence & sources
5. Similarity & difference
6. Society & lifestyle
7. Leadership & government
8. Exploration

---

# 💡 Reality Check

History in FSCE is:

* **NOT knowledge-heavy**
* **VERY vocabulary + reasoning based**

👉 If your child knows the words → easy marks
👉 If not → confusing options in MCQs
