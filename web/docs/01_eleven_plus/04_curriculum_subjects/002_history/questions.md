# History

## 📂 Structure Used

Each topic includes:

* WHY
* WHAT IF
* HOW
* COMPARE
* REAL LIFE
* DECISION

---

# ⏳ 1. CHRONOLOGY & TIMELINES (15 Questions)

1. Why is it important to study history in order?
2. What would happen if we didn’t know when events happened?
3. How do timelines help us understand history?
4. Why do historians divide time into periods?
5. Which is more useful: dates or events? Why?
6. What would happen if timelines were incorrect?
7. How do we measure time in history?
8. Why do some events have more importance than others?
9. What would happen if we mixed up past and present?
10. How can we place events in the correct order?
11. Why do we study ancient before modern history?
12. Which time period would you study first? Why?
13. How do timelines show change over time?
14. What would happen if we ignored timelines completely?
15. Should all history be taught in strict order? Why?

---

# 🏺 2. ANCIENT EGYPT (15 Questions)

1. Why was the Nile important to Ancient Egyptians?
2. What would happen if the Nile didn’t flood one year?
3. How did Egyptians use the Nile in daily life?
4. Why did Egyptians build pyramids?
5. Who had a harder life: farmers or pharaohs? Why?
6. What would happen if pyramids were not built?
7. How did religion affect Egyptian life?
8. Why did Egyptians mummify bodies?
9. What would happen if they didn’t believe in the afterlife?
10. How were Egyptian societies organised?
11. Why were scribes important?
12. Which invention was most important? Why?
13. How did the Nile shape Egypt’s success?
14. What would happen if Egypt had no river?
15. Should we judge Egyptian practices by modern standards? Why?

---

# 🛡️ 3. ROMANS (15 Questions)

1. Why did the Romans build roads?
2. What would happen if Romans hadn’t invaded Britain?
3. How did Roman roads help their empire?
4. Why was the Roman army strong?
5. Who benefited more from Roman rule: rich or poor? Why?
6. What would happen if Roman soldiers were not trained?
7. How did Romans change Britain?
8. Why did Romans build towns and cities?
9. What would happen if Romans didn’t build infrastructure?
10. How did Roman inventions help people?
11. Why did the Roman Empire fall?
12. Which Roman contribution is still useful today? Why?
13. How did trade help the Romans?
14. What would happen if trade stopped?
15. Should the Romans have invaded other countries? Why?

---

# ⚔️ 4. ANGLO-SAXONS & VIKINGS (15 Questions)

1. Why did the Anglo-Saxons come to Britain?
2. What would happen if Vikings never invaded?
3. How did Vikings travel long distances?
4. Why were Viking raids successful?
5. Who were more powerful: Anglo-Saxons or Vikings? Why?
6. What would happen if Anglo-Saxons had stronger defences?
7. How did daily life differ between the two groups?
8. Why did Vikings settle instead of just raid?
9. What would happen if they didn’t settle?
10. How did battles affect people’s lives?
11. Why were kings important?
12. Which group had a better way of life? Why?
13. How did religion influence both groups?
14. What would happen if they never fought?
15. Should Vikings be remembered as invaders or settlers? Why?

---

# 🪨 5. STONE AGE TO IRON AGE (15 Questions)

1. Why did humans move from hunting to farming?
2. What would happen if farming was never discovered?
3. How did early humans survive?
4. Why did tools improve over time?
5. Which age had the biggest change? Why?
6. What would happen if metal was not discovered?
7. How did settlements begin?
8. Why did people stop moving around?
9. What would happen if people stayed as hunter-gatherers?
10. How did life change from Stone Age to Iron Age?
11. Why were new materials important?
12. Which period would be hardest to live in? Why?
13. How did farming affect society?
14. What would happen if crops failed?
15. Should we consider early humans advanced? Why?

---

# 👩‍⚕️ 6. SIGNIFICANT INDIVIDUALS (15 Questions)

(Examples: Florence Nightingale, Mary Seacole)

1. Why was Florence Nightingale important?
2. What would happen if she didn’t improve hospitals?
3. How did she change healthcare?
4. Why is Mary Seacole remembered?
5. Who made a bigger impact? Why?
6. What would happen if no one challenged poor conditions?
7. How did these individuals show courage?
8. Why do we study important people?
9. What would happen if their work was ignored?
10. How do individuals change history?
11. Why were their ideas not accepted at first?
12. Which qualities made them successful?
13. How do their actions affect us today?
14. What would happen if others didn’t follow their ideas?
15. Should individuals or groups get more credit? Why?

---

# 🏠 7. LOCAL HISTORY (10 Questions)

1. Why is local history important?
2. What would happen if we ignored local history?
3. How has your area changed over time?
4. Why do buildings change?
5. Which change has had the biggest impact? Why?
6. What would happen if old buildings were never preserved?
7. How do we find out about local history?
8. Why do people care about heritage?
9. What would happen if traditions disappeared?
10. Should modern buildings replace old ones? Why?

---

# ⚖️ 8. CAUSE & CONSEQUENCE (10 Questions)

1. Why do events happen in history?
2. What would happen if one key event didn’t occur?
3. How do causes lead to consequences?
4. Why do small events sometimes have big effects?
5. Which cause was most important in a given event? Why?
6. What would happen if decisions were different?
7. How do historians identify causes?
8. Why do events have multiple causes?
9. What would happen if we only looked at one cause?
10. Should we focus more on causes or consequences? Why?

---

# 🔍 9. HISTORICAL EVIDENCE & SOURCES (10 Questions)

1. Why do historians use evidence?
2. What would happen if evidence was unreliable?
3. How do artefacts help us understand the past?
4. Why are some sources more trustworthy than others?
5. Which is better: written or physical evidence? Why?
6. What would happen if no evidence existed?
7. How do historians check accuracy?
8. Why can sources be biased?
9. What would happen if we believed all sources?
10. Should we trust everything from the past? Why?

---

# 🔄 10. CHANGE & CONTINUITY (10 Questions)

1. Why do things change over time?
2. What would happen if nothing ever changed?
3. How can we identify change in history?
4. Why do some things stay the same?
5. Which period had the biggest change? Why?
6. What would happen if change happened too quickly?
7. How do inventions drive change?
8. Why do traditions continue?
9. What would happen if traditions disappeared?
10. Should change always be welcomed? Why?

# 🚀 How to Use This (Critical for 11+)

History is where you build **argument skills**:

After each answer, ask:

* “What’s your reason?”
* “Can you give evidence?”
* “Could someone disagree? Why?”

👉 This is exactly what separates top 11+ students
