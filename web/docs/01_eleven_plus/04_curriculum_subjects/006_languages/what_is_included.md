# 🟩 Core Skill Areas for FSCE Language Section

---

## 1. 🔍 Pattern Recognition (VERY HIGH PRIORITY)

### Concept

Words follow hidden rules — child must identify them.

---

### Example

* “mip” = dog
* “mipla” = dogs

👉 Rule: **“la” = plural**

---

### Likely Question

* What does “catla” mean? → **cats**

---

## 2. 🔤 Symbol / Word Mapping

### Concept

Match unknown words to meanings.

---

### Example

* “zor” = sun
* “pel zor” = hot sun

👉 “pel” = hot

---

### Likely Question

* What does “pel” mean? → **hot**

---

## 3. ➕ Adding Endings / Prefixes

### Concept

Words change meaning with added parts.

---

### Example

* “dak” = run
* “daking” = running

👉 “ing” = happening now

---

### Likely Questions

* What does “jumping” mean? → **happening now**
* What does suffix show? → **change in meaning**

---

## 4. 🔄 Sentence Decoding

### Concept

Understand meaning of whole sentences.

---

### Example

* “mip pel” = dog hot
  👉 rearranged → **hot dog**

---

### Likely Questions

* What does the sentence mean? (MCQ)

---

## 5. 🧩 Missing Word / Fill the Gap

### Concept

Use pattern to complete sentence.

---

### Example

* “mip = dog”
* “cat = fel”
* “dogs = mipla”

👉 What is “cats”? → **fella**

---

---

## 6. 🔀 Odd One Out

### Concept

Find which word breaks the pattern.

---

### Example

* mip, mipla, miplo, fel

👉 “fel” doesn’t follow pattern

---

---

## 7. 🔁 Reversing Meaning

### Concept

Translate both directions.

---

### Example

* “dak” = run

👉 What is “run” in code? → **dak**

---

---

## 8. 🧠 Logical Elimination (VERY IMPORTANT)

### Concept

Use options to figure out meaning even if unsure.

---

### Example

* Only one option fits all rules → choose that

---

---

# 🧩 Skills to Build (Not Vocabulary)

This section is NOT about memorising words.

Teach skills:

* spotting patterns
* comparing words
* breaking words into parts
* testing rules
* eliminating wrong answers

---

# 🎯 What FSCE Will Actually Test

They will give:

* a **made-up language**
* a few examples
* then ask:

✔ What does this word mean?
✔ Which sentence is correct?
✔ What is the rule?
✔ Complete the pattern

---

# 🧠 How to Train This (Very Important)

This is where most people go wrong.

Don’t teach:
❌ vocabulary lists
❌ real languages

---

### Do this instead:

Practice like puzzles:

#### Type 1: Code breaking

* “dak = run”
* “daklo = runs”
  👉 What does “lo” mean?

---

#### Type 2: Pattern extension

* cat → fel
* dog → mip
  👉 bird → ? (from options)

---

#### Type 3: Rule finding

* mip → dog
* mipla → dogs
  👉 what does “la” mean?

---

# 🔑 Final Structure (What to Teach)

Teach Languages as **reasoning skills**:

1. Pattern recognition
2. Word mapping
3. Prefix/suffix meaning
4. Sentence decoding
5. Fill-in patterns
6. Odd one out
7. Reverse translation
8. Logical elimination
