### üìò Explanation:

A **table** is a way of showing information in **rows** and **columns**, so it‚Äôs easy to compare and find facts quickly.
You often see tables in **timetables, survey results, shop lists, or data summaries**.

Tables help you read, compare, and calculate ‚Äî for example, adding totals or finding differences between rows.

---

### üîπ Key ideas

* **Rows** go across ‚Üí show categories or time periods
* **Columns** go down ‚Üí show details, numbers, or labels
* **Headings** tell you what each row and column means
* Tables can show **totals**, **averages**, or **comparisons**
* Always **read the labels carefully** before answering questions

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Common mistakes

* Misreading the wrong row or column
* Ignoring totals or subtotals
* Mixing units (e.g. ¬£ vs p, cm vs m)
* Forgetting to check if the question asks for *difference*, *total*, or *average*

#### Example 4 ‚Äì Missing value
