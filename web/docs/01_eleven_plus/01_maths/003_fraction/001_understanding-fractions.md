# Understanding Fractions

A **fraction** is a way of showing a part of something. Think of a fraction as a pizza that‚Äôs been sliced up.

* The **top number** (called the **numerator**) tells us **how many parts we have**.
* The **bottom number** (called the **denominator**) tells us **how many equal parts the whole is split into**.

Example:
In $\tfrac{3}{4}$ the **3** means we have 3 parts, and the **4** means the whole is split into 4 parts. So $\tfrac{3}{4}$ is **3 out of 4 equal parts**.

### üé® Visual Metaphor:

Imagine a chocolate bar split into 8 pieces. If you eat 5, you‚Äôve eaten $\tfrac{5}{8}$ of it!

### üß† Key Ideas:

* Fractions are about **equal parts**.
* Every whole number can be written as a fraction. (Example: $1 = \tfrac{2}{2}$ or $\tfrac{3}{3}$)
* Fractions can be **proper** (less than 1) like $\tfrac{3}{4}$ or **improper** (more than 1) like $\tfrac{7}{4}$.
* An **improper fraction** can also be written as a **mixed number**. (Example: $\tfrac{7}{4} = 1\tfrac{3}{4}$)

### üßÆ Examples:

1. üçï A pizza is cut into 6 slices. You eat 2. What fraction did you eat? ‚Üí $\tfrac{2}{6}$
2. üßÉ A juice bottle is filled up to $\tfrac{3}{5}$. What part is empty? ‚Üí $\tfrac{2}{5}$
3. üß© Write 1 whole as a fraction with 4 as the denominator. ‚Üí $\tfrac{4}{4}$
4. üç´ You eat $1\tfrac{1}{2}$ chocolate bars. Write this as a fraction. ‚Üí $\tfrac{3}{2}$

üí° **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

* Don‚Äôt confuse numerator and denominator.
* Fractions must be of **equal parts** ‚Äî 2 out of 5 *unequal* slices isn't $\tfrac{2}{5}$!

### üß© Word Problems:

#### üîπ Easy

1. Sam eats 3 slices out of a pizza cut into 8 equal slices. What fraction did he eat?
2. A bottle has 10 equal parts of water. You drink 4. What fraction is gone?
3. Lily paints 2 out of 5 fence panels. What fraction is painted?

9. A glass is filled up to $\tfrac{1}{2}$. How much is still empty?
10. A pack of cards has 10 cards. You take 7. What fraction have you taken?

#### üî∏ Medium

11. A pie is cut into 8 equal slices. Joe eats 5 and gives away 2. What fraction is left?

14. A rope is 5 metres long. 2 metres are coloured red. What fraction is coloured?
15. If a jar holds 10 litres and is filled to $\tfrac{3}{5}$, how much is filled?
16. A rectangle is divided into 6 parts. 2 are shaded blue, 1 yellow, rest white. What fraction is white?
17. A baker cuts 3 cakes into quarters. He sells 5 quarters. What fraction did he sell?
18. A fruit basket has 5 bananas, 4 apples, and 1 orange. What fraction are bananas?
19. A class votes for a game: 6 for football, 4 for cricket, 2 for tag. What fraction picked cricket?
20. A book has 120 pages. John has read $\tfrac{1}{3}$. How many pages has he read?

#### üî∫ Hard

24. In a quiz, $\tfrac{1}{2}$ of the questions are maths, $\tfrac{1}{3}$ science, and rest English. What fraction are English?
25. A jug holds $3\tfrac{3}{4}$ litres. You pour out $1\tfrac{1}{2}$ litres. What fraction remains?
26. A baker makes 4 trays of cupcakes. Each tray has 12. He sells $\tfrac{3}{4}$ of them. How many left?
27. A rectangle has 12 equal parts. $\tfrac{1}{3}$ are blue, $\tfrac{1}{4}$ red. What fraction is not coloured?
28. There are 36 students: $\tfrac{1}{6}$ play piano, $\tfrac{1}{3}$ play guitar, rest play nothing. How many play nothing?
29. You jogged $\tfrac{2}{5}$ of a 10 km path and walked $\tfrac{2}{5}$. What fraction of the path is still to go?
30. A glass is filled with juice to $\tfrac{7}{8}$. You drink $\tfrac{3}{8}$. What fraction remains?
