# Dividing Fractions

### üìò Explanation:

To **divide fractions**, we flip the second fraction and **multiply**.
This is known as **"multiply by the reciprocal."**

> **Reciprocal** means flipping a fraction.
> Example: The reciprocal of $\tfrac{2}{3}$ is **$\tfrac{3}{2}$**

---

### üß† Steps to Divide Fractions:

1. **Keep** the first fraction as it is.
2. **Flip** the second fraction (find the reciprocal).
3. **Multiply** the two fractions.
4. **Simplify** if needed.

> Example:
> $\tfrac{2}{3}$ √∑ $\tfrac{4}{5}$
> ‚Üí $\tfrac{2}{3}$ √ó $\tfrac{5}{4}$ = $\tfrac{10}{12}$ = **$\tfrac{5}{6}$**

---

### üî¢ Dividing by Whole Numbers

Write the whole number as a fraction over 1, then follow the same rule.

> Example:
> $\tfrac{3}{4}$ √∑ 2 = $\tfrac{3}{4}$ √∑ $\tfrac{2}{1}$ = $\tfrac{3}{4}$ √ó $\tfrac{1}{2}$ = **$\tfrac{3}{8}$**

---

### üçï Real-Life Analogy:

You have **$\tfrac{2}{3}$ of a pizza** and want to split it evenly between **2 people**.
You do:
**$\tfrac{2}{3}$ √∑ 2 = $\tfrac{1}{3}$** ‚Äî each person gets **$\tfrac{1}{3}$ of the whole pizza**.

---

### üßÆ Examples:

1. $\tfrac{1}{2}$ √∑ $\tfrac{1}{4}$ ‚Üí $\tfrac{1}{2}$ √ó $\tfrac{4}{1}$ = **$\tfrac{4}{2}$ = 2**
2. $\tfrac{3}{5}$ √∑ $\tfrac{1}{2}$ ‚Üí $\tfrac{3}{5}$ √ó $\tfrac{2}{1}$ = **$\tfrac{6}{5}$ = 1 $\tfrac{1}{5}$**
3. 4 √∑ $\tfrac{1}{2}$ = $\tfrac{4}{1}$ √ó $\tfrac{2}{1}$ = **8**
4. $\tfrac{3}{4}$ √∑ 3 = $\tfrac{3}{4}$ √ó $\tfrac{1}{3}$ = **$\tfrac{1}{4}$**
5. $\tfrac{5}{6}$ √∑ $\tfrac{2}{3}$ ‚Üí $\tfrac{5}{6}$ √ó $\tfrac{3}{2}$ = **$\tfrac{15}{12}$ = 1 $\tfrac{1}{4}$**
